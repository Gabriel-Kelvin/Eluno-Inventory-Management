# Welcome to Eluno: Your Complete Guide

Hello there! Welcome to **Eluno**, an AI-Powered Eyewear Operations Platform. 

If you're new to the optical business or just want to understand how this software works, you are in the right place! Think of Eluno as the "brain" behind an eyewear store or a lens manufacturing lab. It helps track customer orders, manage the physical stock of lenses, and uses Artificial Intelligence (AI) to predict if an order might be delayed.

Let's break down everything from A to Z in very simple, beginner-friendly terms.

---

## 1. What is the Purpose of this App?

When a customer walks into an eyewear store, gets an eye test, and orders a pair of glasses, a lot of things have to happen behind the scenes:
1. The store needs to check if they have the right lenses in stock.
2. If they do, the lenses need to be cut, coated, and fitted into the frame.
3. If they don't, they have to order the lenses from a supplier.
4. The customer expects their glasses in a few days (this promise is called the **SLA** - Service Level Agreement).

**The purpose of Eluno is to manage this entire journey.** It ensures lenses are always in stock, tracks the progress of every single customer order, and uses AI to warn the staff *before* an order gets delayed so the customer is never disappointed.

## 2. Understanding the Data We Use

Before we look at the tabs, let's understand the data you will see in the app. Eyewear has a lot of specific terms. Here is what they mean:

### Eye Prescription Data (The Power)
- **SPH (Sphere):** This is the main "power" of the lens. If the number is negative (like -2.00), the person is nearsighted (can't see far). If it's positive (+2.00), they are farsighted (can't see near).
- **CYL (Cylinder):** This power corrects "Astigmatism" (when the eye is shaped a bit like a football instead of a perfect sphere, causing blurry vision at all distances).
- **Axis:** If someone has a CYL power, the Axis (a number from 1 to 180) tells the machine the exact angle to place that CYL power on the lens.

### Lens Details
- **Lens Type:** 
  - *Single Vision:* A standard lens with one power all over. Used for reading OR distance.
  - *Bifocal:* A lens with two powers (usually a small visible half-circle at the bottom for reading, and the top for distance).
  - *Progressive:* A modern lens with multiple powers that smoothly transition from top to bottom (no visible lines).
- **Lens Index:** This is the thickness/thinness of the lens. A higher index (like 1.67 or 1.74) means the lens is much thinner and lighter, which is great for people with high powers.
- **Coating:** Special layers added to the lens.
  - *Anti-Glare (Anti-Reflective):* Stops annoying reflections from lights and screens.
  - *Blue Cut:* Blocks blue light from digital screens like phones and computers to reduce eye strain.
  - *Photochromic (Transitions):* Lenses that turn dark like sunglasses when you go outside into the sun.

### Operational Data
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** The maximum number of days promised to the customer to deliver their glasses.
- **TAT (Turnaround Time):** The actual time it takes to finish the glasses.
- **QC (Quality Check):** A step where a human or machine checks if the glasses are perfect before giving them to the customer.

## 3. Explaining the Sidebar Tabs

Here is a breakdown of every tab you see on the left side of your screen:

### 🛡️ Command Center
**What it is:** The "Control Room" of your operations. 
**Why it's there:** It gives managers a bird's-eye view of the most critical alerts. If something is broken, a machine is down, or a massive delay is happening, it shows up here.

### 🎛️ Dashboard
**What it is:** The daily summary screen.
**Why it's there:** It shows beautiful charts and numbers summarizing how the business is doing today. You can see how many orders were placed, how many were delivered, and how much inventory you have left. It's used to get a quick pulse of the business.

### 📋 Orders
**What it is:** The tracking system for every pair of glasses.
**Why it's there:** Every time a customer buys glasses, an "Order" is created. This tab tracks the glasses as they move through different stages: *Placed -> Lens Allocation -> Lab Processing -> Coating -> Quality Check -> Shipping -> Delivered.* You use this to find out exactly where a customer's glasses are at any given second.

### ⚠️ Risk Center
**What it is:** The AI-powered crystal ball.
**Why it's there:** Sometimes machines break, or suppliers are late. The Risk Center uses AI to look at all current orders and predict *which orders are going to be late* (SLA Breaches). It gives each order a "Risk Score." This allows the staff to jump in and fix problems before the customer even knows there was a delay!

### 📦 Inventory
**What it is:** The digital warehouse.
**Why it's there:** Lenses come in thousands of combinations (different SPH, CYL, Types, Coatings). The Inventory tab keeps count of exactly how many lenses of each specific combination are sitting on the physical shelves. If a specific lens drops to "0", it shows as "Out of Stock."

### 📈 Forecasting
**What it is:** The future planner.
**Why it's there:** Running out of lenses is bad for business. The Forecasting tab looks at past sales and uses AI to guess which lenses you will need *next month*. It tells you, "Hey, a lot of people buy -2.00 Single Vision lenses in the summer, you should order 50 more right now."

### ✨ AI Copilot
**What it is:** Your personal AI assistant (like ChatGPT, but strictly for your store).
**Why it's there:** Instead of clicking through tabs to find answers, a manager can just type: *"How many orders are delayed today?"* or *"Do we have enough Blue Cut lenses for tomorrow?"* The Copilot reads the database and answers instantly in plain English.

## Summary
In short, **Eluno** takes the chaotic, physical process of making custom glasses and turns it into a smooth, smart, and AI-driven digital workflow. It ensures the right lenses are always in the **Inventory**, tracks them in the **Orders** tab, uses the **Forecasting** tab to buy more in advance, and uses the **Risk Center** to make sure the customer gets their glasses on time, every time!
